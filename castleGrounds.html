CASTLE GROUNDS RECREATION

This is a recreation of the Castle Grounds area in Super Mario 64 using OpenGL. This was created as a final project for a computer graphics class at CU Boulder. This class taught me the basics in how computer graphics works and how OpenGL can be used to create models and scenes with lighting and textures.

I think I did a good job at recreating the level. I tried to put in as much detail as I was able to. I am proud of my tri and quad functions that I created for this project. These functions act as a replacement for the GL_TRIANGLE_STRIP and GL_QUAD_STRIP functions that OpenGL uses. My new functions take an array of vertices and creates the shapes I want while also calculating the proper surface normals and applying the correct textures. I am also proud of the overall result of the project. Being that I was going for a replication I needed specific details at specific locations. So, I ended up drawing a map of all of the vertices in the scene on a large piece of graph paper which I then converted to multiple arrays of vertices in the program.
